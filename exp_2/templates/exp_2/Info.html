Your chain’s market research division is tasked with providing you with the information necessary to make good wholesale and retail pricing decisions for your company.

Supplier Cost Information:

The research division has found that the average production cost of all roasted coffee suppliers is 6 dollars per pound. However, any particular supplier’s actual production cost is private information. Research reports that it could be anywhere between 2 and 10 dollars per pound with equal likelihood.

Consumer Information:

Similarly, the research division has found that on average a retail customer is willing to pay 6 dollars per cup of high quality coffee. However, some customers will pay much more, or less, than this amount. Research reports that retail customers are willing to pay anywhere between 2 and 10 dollars per cup, with equal likeliness.

Note: Supplier cost is given per pound but consumer willingness to pay is given per cup. Your firm converts one pound of roasted beans produces retail-ready 28 cups of coffee.

You should take this information into account when choosing a wholesale price (W) and a retail price (R).
