Market Research Information:

Your chain’s market research division is tasked with providing you with the information necessary to make good wholesale and retail pricing decisions for your company.

Supplier Cost Information:

  • The research division has found that the average production cost of all roasted coffee suppliers is normally distributed with an average cost of $6 per pound.
  • However, any particular supplier’s actual production cost is private information. Research reports that it is normally distributed with an average cost of $6 per pound and standard deviation of $0.5.

Consumer Information:

  • Similarly, the research division has found that on average a retail customer is willing to pay 4 dollars per cup of high quality coffee. However, some customers are willing to pay much more, or less, than this amount.
  • Research reports that retail customers are willing to pay anywhere between 1 and 7 dollars per cup, with equal possibility.
  • Note: Supplier cost is given per pound but consumer willingness to pay is given per cup. Your firm uses one pound of roasted beans produces 28 cups of retail-ready coffee.

You should take this information into account when choosing a wholesale price (W) or a retail price (R).
